Titles

Premier League

Total: 20


  • star 1907-08 United went 23-6-9 with 52 points and 81 goals scored. Ernest Mangnall led the team and had an average attendance of 22,901. Sandy Turnbull would lead the league in scoring with 27 goals.
  • star 1910-11 The Red Devils dramatically won the league by one point over Aston Villa. United once again had the teams's top scorer with Enoch West scoring 19 league goals.
  • star 1951-52 Sir Matt Busby and the team scored 95 goals on the season. 30 of them came from the league's top scorer, Jack Rowley. United had an average of 41,374 fans come to Old Trafford.
  • star 1955-56 United ran away with the league after winning it by 11 points over Blackpool. Tommy Taylor was the best goal scorer in the league and he netted 25 on the season.
  • star 1956-57 The team scored 103 goals on their way to the league title. Irishman Billy Whelan scored 26 league goals to lead the charge.
  • star 1964-65 Manchester managed to win the league on goal difference over Leeds United. They only allowed 39 goals on the season and that matched Denis Law's goalscoring total that year.
  • star 1966-67 Busby's boys went 24-12-6 in the league and beat out Nottingham Forest. Denis Law was once again the tallysman with 25 on the season.
  • star 1992-93 Sir Alex Ferguson led the Red Devils to their first league title in 26 years. Mark Hughes was the leading scorer with 15 and Ryan Giggs added 11.
  • star 1993-94 United won the double by outdoing Blackburn Rovers in the league and defeating Chelsea in the FA Cup Final. Cantona was at his best with 25 goals on the season.
  • star 1995-96 Sir Alex Ferguson dramatically led the comeback charge when Newcastle United looked destined to win the league. They won seven of their last eight games to capture the title.
  • star 1996-97 United were in first place from Februrary of that year and never let go of their lead. Ole Gunnar Solskjaer was the team's top scorer with 19 goals.
  • star 1998-99 The magical treble winning season where they won the league by one point over Arsenal having lost by the same deficit the year prior. Dwight Yorke netted 29 goals overall.
  • star 1999-00 Arguably United's most dominant season to this point. They won the league by 18 points and had five players score at least 12 goals or more.
  • star 2000-01 The Red Devils were incredibly balanced again and only allowed 31 goals. Teddy Sheringham got back to his usual self scoring 21 goals in all competitions.
  • star 2002-03 United managed to edge out Arsenal by five points in the league. Ruud Van Nistelrooy was unstoppable with 44 goals just 50 appearances.
  • star 2006-07 This was the year of Cristiano Ronaldo and Wayne Rooney. Both scored 23 goals in all competitions to lead the team to it's 16th English League Title.
  • star 2007-08 United had an impeccable defensive record thanks to the likes of Edwin van der Sar and Rio Ferdinand. Just 22 goals were allowed and Cristiano Ronaldo also scored 42 goals overall.
  • star 2008-09 The Red Devils edged out Liverpool to win the title. Cristiano Ronaldo, Wayne Rooney, Carlos Tevez and Dimitar Berbatov were too good.
  • star 2010-11 Sir Alex Ferguson's men won the league by nine points over Chelsea and Manchester City. Dimitar Berbatov and Chicarito Hernandez did most of the scoring, combining for 40 league goals.
  • star 2012-13 The year Sir Alex fleeced Arsene Wenger to purchase Robin van Persie from Arsenal. The Dutchman would score 30 goals in all competitions to seal the 20th Premier League title.

Cups

Total: 20

  • star FA Cup: 1908-09 United defeated Bristol City 1-0 in the Final while beating teams like Newcastle United, Everton and Blackburn Rovers along the way.
  • star FA Cup: 1947-48 A Jack Rowley double saw the Red Devils beat Blackpool 4-2 in the Final at Wembley. 99,842 were in attendence to see United's second FA Cup win.
  • star FA Cup: 1962-63 Matt Busby's boys were dominant over Leicester City with a 3-1 win and brace from David Herd. United were able to exact revenge after Leicester had just done the double over them.
  • star Champions League: 1967-68 United's first Champions League title came after thrashing Benfica 4-1. Sir Bobby Charlton scored twice and George Best added one as well. The Red Devils also defeated Real Madrid in the semis.
  • star FA Cup: 1976-77 The club was able to prevent Liverpool from winning an unprecedented treble with a 2-1 win. All three goals were scored within four minutes.
  • star FA Cup: 1982-83 United and Bristol City played to a 2-2 draw in the first match but the Red Devils reigned supreme in the Replay. The match ended 4-0 with a double for Bryan Robson.
  • star FA Cup: 1984-85 The team's sixth FA Cup would be sealed with a 1-0 win in extra time. Norman Whiteside scored in the 110th minute to secure the victory.
  • star FA Cup: 1989-90 A late Mark Hughes goal in extra time against Crystal Palace made it 3-3 to force a replay. They would win the replay 1-0 with a goal from left back Lee Martin.
  • star Football League Cup: 1991-92 Brian McClair would score the game's only goal and United won their first League Cup at the hands of Nottingham Forest.
  • star FA Cup: 1993-94 United thrashed Chelsea 4-0 in the final with two penalties in six minutes from Eric Cantona in the second half. McClair and Hughes added a goal each as well.
  • star FA Cup: 1995-96 Two of the more attacking sides in the league would neutralize each other until the 85th minute. Eric Cantona would score the winner in a magical moment, 1-0 was the final score.
  • star FA Cup: 1998-99 United defeated both Chelsea and Arsenal to get the final. Paul Scholes and Teddy Sheringham both scored to secure the club's 10th FA Cup by beating Newcastle United.
  • star Champions League: 1998-99 A miracle of all miracles, United were trailed the entire game 1-0. This was until two late stoppage time goals from Sheringham and Solskjaer to win the cup and the treble.
  • star FA Cup: 2003-04 United easily defeated Cinderella story Millwall by a 3-0 scoreline with a goal from Cristiano Ronaldo and double from Ruud van Nistelrooy.
  • star Football League Cup: 2005-06 The Red Devils had too much firepower for Wigan Athletic in this one. Wayne Rooney scored a double with Cristiano Ronaldo and Louis Saha adding a goal each.
  • star Champions League: 2007-08 This was a dramatic match between United and Chelsea that saw both sides fight to penalties. John Terry slipped and his shot hit the post to win the game. Edwin van der Sar was brilliant.
  • star Football League Cup: 2008-09 A 0-0 match then went to penalties but wasn't even close in the end. The Red Devils would win the shootout and cup, 4-1 over Tottenham Hotspur.
  • star Football League Cup: 2009-10 After Aston Villa took an early lead, it would be Michael Owen and Wayne Rooney who saved the day for Manchester United. Final score was 2-1.
  • star FA Cup: 2015-16 Alan Pardew's dancing celebration caught everyone's eye after Crystal Palace scored to take the lead. Yet it was Jesse Lingard's strike that stole the show and won it for United in extra time.
  • star Football League Cup: 2016-17 Zlatan won the cup for United thanks to a double. After giving up a two goal lead, Zlatan bailed out the Red Devils with an 87th minute goal.
